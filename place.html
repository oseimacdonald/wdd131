About Ghana

Ghana is a West African country known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and diverse landscapes. It gained independence from British colonial rule in 1957, becoming the first sub-Saharan African nation to achieve independence. Accra, the capital city, is a bustling urban center and a hub of culture, commerce, and government. Ghana is renowned for its traditional Ashanti and other indigenous cultures, vibrant music and dance, and welcoming hospitality. The country is also celebrated for its natural beauty, including expansive savannahs, tropical rainforests, and beautiful coastlines along the Gulf of Guinea. Ghana's economy is one of the strongest in Africa, driven by agriculture, mining (especially gold), and increasingly, services and technology sectors.
