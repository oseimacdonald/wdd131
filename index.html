Official Flag of Ghana

Capital: Accra

History: Ghana, located in West Africa, has a rich history that spans several centuries. Originally inhabited by various ethnic groups, Ghana became a powerful empire known as the Ghana Empire around the 8th century. It was renowned for its wealth and trade in gold and salt.

In the 15th century, Portuguese explorers arrived and established trading posts along the coast. The area later became known as the Gold Coast due to its abundant gold resources.

Ghana played a significant role in the transatlantic slave trade, with European powers establishing forts and trading in enslaved Africans. In the 19th century, the British gradually colonized the region, leading to the formation of the British Gold Coast colony.

Ghana gained independence from British colonial rule on March 6, 1957, becoming the first sub-Saharan African nation to achieve independence. Kwame Nkrumah, Ghana's first president, played a crucial role in the independence movement and Pan-Africanism.
